the challenge

Redesign the password generator for 1password.

1password faces a problem that lots of their users use their weak password all the time.
Though 1password offers a random strong password generator, users seldomly use it.

discover the problem

why do people keep saving simple passwords into the vault?

Meet our personas

Robert

Male | 50s | Retired Officer

Low tech ability. Store important information into the 1Password vault. Concern highly about information security.

"I want it secure and under my control."

Lisa

Female | 20s | Freelancer

Social media enthusiast. Tired of remembering passwords. Does not know much about computer security.

"I use all the popular social media. Every day!"

How do people use their password?

What's wrong with the current design?

  1. Hard to find the generator

    Password generator is hiding inside the tool bar icon.

  2. Totally random password

    Password generated is totally random, which is robotic and cold for users.

  3. Lack of notification

    There is no notification about weak password.

insights

trust

People do not trust a password generated by a program. They feel unfamiliar with the password, and feel lose of control.

fear

People are afraid of using a totally random password. They want to have a sense of what the password means.

tedious

They current process of strenthen a password is tedious. It's hard to find the way to make changes and save a secure password.

our design

a better user flow and a more trustful generator

  • Keep everything within the browser

    Instead of going to the native app from website back and forth, our design provide notification and generator inside the browser.

  • Guide the user to next step

    Our design can notify the user weak password, help the user use the generator, and guide the user to change the password.

  1. Show the strength of password

    User can easily notice the strength of current password. This strength bar will also appear when the user types password.

  2. Generate passphrase based on the input

    The generator creates passphrase user is familiar with, by randomizing user's input.

  3. Customization of the generator

    User can control the customization process.

  4. Color matching to ensure the familiarity

    The final passphrase use the same color coding with the words user typed in, to reensure the familiarity.

Reflection

I am satisfied with most parts of this project. It was a complete process from exploring the problem space, getting to know the user, to designing & testing iterations.

I think usability tests and iterations after testing still have much space to improved. We tried to use Microsoft RITE usability testing method, which is basically iterating right after each test. However, we kind of used this method in a wrong way. We were to focused on the participants' reaction of the prototype, we made some changes without deeply thinking about why they had such reaction. We were led by what the participants did and said.

One more thing is the use of paper prototype in usability testing. The nature of paper prototype made it easy for participants find the interactive part and hard to understand the screen flow. I think paper prototype is great for an early stage of design because of its efficiency. However, in the later stage of design process, a higher-fidelity prototype would be better.
